Best Things To Do In Kilifi County

Kilifi County is a county in kenya, located on the coast of the Indian Ocean. It has a population of about 1.5 million people and covers an area of about 12,246 sq.kilometers.

It's a coastal county in Kenya that offers a variety of attractions and activities for visitors, home to beautiful beaches, historical sites, cultural heritage, and natural wonders. Whether you are looking for relaxation, adventure, or education, this haven surely has something for you.

It is one of the five counties that make up the Kenyan Coast.

The current governor of Kilifi County as of 2022 is Gideon Mung'aro.

It has a rich history and culture influenced by the Swahili, Arab, Portugese and British civilizations.

These are some of the top 10 things to do in Kilifi County that you should not miss when you visit this amazing county. You will surely have a memorable and enjoyable time exploring the beauty, culture, and adventure that Kilifi County has to offer.

    Relax at Bofa Beach in Kilifi

    Bofa Beach is one of the most beautiful and serene beaches in Kenya, with white sand, clear water, and palm trees. It is ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying the breeze. You can also find some hotels and resorts nearby that offer accommodation and amenities.

    Stay at Distant Relatives Eco Lodge.

    Distant Relatives is a unique and eco-friendly lodge that offers a comfortable and communal stay for travelers. It is located in a lush garden near Kilifi Creek, where you can enjoy activities such as kayaking, sailing, fishing, and snorkeling. The lodge also organizes events such as live music, camping, yoga, workshops, and parties.

    Visit the Rabai Museum.

    Rabai Museum is a historical site that showcases the history and culture of the Mijikenda people, who are the indigenous inhabitants of the coastal region. The museum is housed in a former missionary station that was built in 1846 by the first Christian missionaries in Kenya. You can learn about the Mijikenda traditions, religion, art, and crafts

    Explore Mekatilili Wa Menza Site.

    Mekatilili wa Menza was a legendary female freedom fighter who led the Giriama people against the British colonial rule in the early 20th century. She is revered as a national hero and a symbol of resistance and empowerment for women. You can visit her burial site and memorial statue in Magarini, where she died in 1925.

    Explore the coastal nightlife in Watamu and Mtwapa.

    Watamu and Mtwapa are two popular towns in Kilifi County that offer a vibrant and lively nightlife scene. You can find many bars, clubs, restaurants, and entertainment venues that cater to different tastes and preferences. You can enjoy live music, dance, karaoke, comedy, and more.

    Go for thrilling adventures (Kitesurfing and Snorkeling).

    Kilifi County is a paradise for adventure seekers who want to experience some adrenaline-pumping activities on the water. You can try kitesurfing, which is a sport that combines surfing, windsurfing, and paragliding. You can glide on the waves using a kite and a board, while enjoying the wind and the scenery2. You can also try snorkeling, which is a sport that allows you to explore the underwater world using a mask, a snorkel, and fins. You can see colorful fish, coral reefs, turtles, dolphins, and other marine life

    Get a historical experience at Malindi Museum.

    Malindi Museum is a museum that displays the rich and diverse history of Malindi town, which is one of the oldest towns in Kenya. Malindi was an important port and trading center that attracted merchants and explorers from different parts of the world, such as Arabs, Indians, Portuguese, Chinese, and British. You can see exhibits and artifacts that reflect the cultural and historical influences of these civilizations.

    Visit Mida Creek.

    Mida Creek is a large tidal inlet that forms part of the Watamu Marine National Park and Reserve. It is home to a variety of flora and fauna, such as mangroves, crabs, birds, fish, monkeys, and crocodiles. You can enjoy activities such as birdwatching, canoeing, fishing, and walking on the boardwalk.

    Visit Vasco Da Gama Pillar.

    Vasco Da Gama Pillar is a historical monument that was erected by the Portuguese explorer Vasco Da Gama in 1498 during his voyage to India. It is one of the oldest European monuments in Africa and a landmark of Malindi town. It consists of a stone pillar with a cross on top that stands on a coral cliff overlooking the Indian Ocean.
